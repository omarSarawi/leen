Jerash - Ancient City in Jordan

Experience the grandeur of Jerash, an ancient city nestled in the heart of northern Jordan. With its majestic ruins and well-preserved architecture, Jerash offers a captivating glimpse into the past.

Wander through its storied streets and marvel at the towering columns and impressive amphitheaters that speak volumes about its rich history. From the Roman Empire to the present day, Jerash has stood as a testament to the enduring spirit of humanity.

The History of Jerash

Jerash, also known as Gerasa, was founded in the 4th century BC and rose to prominence during the Hellenistic period. However, it wasn't until the Roman conquest in 63 BC that Jerash truly flourished as a major city in the Decapolis league.

Under Roman rule, Jerash thrived as a center of trade and commerce, boasting impressive infrastructure and architectural marvels. The city reached its peak during the 2nd century AD, with a population of over 20,000 inhabitants.
